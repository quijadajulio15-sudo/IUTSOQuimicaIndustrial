Medidas y cantidades

Verbo "To Be" — Mini clase

Resumen: El verbo "to be" (am / is / are / was / were) se usa para identificar (I am a technician), describir estados (The solution is hot) y ubicar (They are in the lab). Negación: add "not" (is not / are not) o contracciones (isn't / aren't). A continuación verás bloques con una breve explicación y ejercicios interactivos.

Presente — Afirmativo

Sujeto + am/is/are — Ejemplos: I am a chemist. The sample is ready. We are on shift.

Presente — Negativo

Sujeto + am/is/are + not — Ej.: The reactor isn't ready.

Pasado — Afirmativo

Sujeto + was/were — Ej.: I was at the inspection. They were on duty.

Pasado — Negativo

Sujeto + was/were + not — Ej.: The machine wasn't calibrated.

Verbos Modales — Clase

Mini clase: Modal verbs express ability, permission, obligation and possibility. - Can / Could: ability or permission (Operators can close the valve). - Must: strong obligation (You must wear PPE). - Should: advice/recommendation (You should follow SOP). - May / Might: probability/possibility (This may be hazardous).

Gerundio (-ING) — Clase

Mini clase: Use -ing to talk about actions in progress (He is mixing), after prepositions (before adding), and as nouns (Mixing reagents is important).

Pronombres Posesivos — Clase

Mini clase: Indicate ownership: my/your/his/her/its/our/their (adjetivos) and mine/yours/his/hers/ours/theirs (pronombres). Ej.: "This is our lab." / "The report is theirs".
